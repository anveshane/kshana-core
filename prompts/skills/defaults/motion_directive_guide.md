**PURPOSE**: Rewrite a shot's description into a concise motion directive optimized for LTX video generation. The output replaces the raw scene description as the text prompt for video generation.

---

## What You Receive

- The shot's visual description and camera work from the shot planner
- The world style bible for consistency

## What You Must Produce

A single paragraph, max 50 words. Camera movement first, then subject motion, then atmosphere.

## Rules

- **Action verbs only** — "dolly forward", "rack focus", "tilt up", not "the camera shows"
- **Physical motion** — describe what MOVES and HOW, not what it means narratively
- **No backstory** — not "the investigator, haunted by his past, walks" → "figure walks slowly through vapor"
- **No emotions** — not "a sense of dread" → "shadows lengthen, light flickers"
- **Camera first** — lead with camera movement if any
- **Concise** — every word must add visual or motion information

## Examples

**BAD** (narrative prose — too long, too abstract):
"The investigator, woken from centuries of cryo-sleep, slowly opens his eyes as the harsh light of the sterile waking room floods his vision, creating a sense of disorientation and vulnerability."

**GOOD** (motion directive — concise, physical):
"Static close-up. Frost shatters from eyelashes. Eyes snap open, pupils contracting. Rack focus from face to blinding overhead light."

**BAD**: "She feels overwhelmed by the weight of her responsibilities as she scrubs."
**GOOD**: "Hands drive rag into stone tile, rhythmic scrubbing. Sweat drops from brow. Pace accelerates."

**BAD**: "The empty city stretches out below, a monument to humanity's failure."
**GOOD**: "Slow pull-back from window glass. Cityscape resolves through condensation. No movement in the streets below."

Output ONLY the motion directive — no explanation, no labels.
