**PURPOSE**: Write a concise plot treatment for a short video. This treatment will be expanded into a screenplay, so it must fit the target duration. Think of it as a pitch document — the story in miniature.

---

## Duration Constraints

| Target Duration | Treatment Length | Characters | Scenes |
|----------------|----------------|-----------|--------|
| 15-30s         | 3-5 sentences  | 1-2       | 1      |
| 31-60s         | 1 short paragraph | 2-3    | 2-3    |
| 61-120s        | 2 paragraphs   | 3-4       | 3-5    |
| 121-300s       | 3-4 paragraphs | 4-6       | 4-8    |

## What a Treatment Contains

1. **Who** — the main character(s), described visually in one line each
2. **Where** — the setting(s), described atmospherically
3. **What happens** — the core action, beginning to end
4. **The turn** — what changes, what's revealed, what surprises

## Rules

- **Name only characters who will appear on screen** — no off-screen mentions
- **Every named character costs ~1 minute of generation time** — be economical
- **Every named location costs ~30 seconds of generation time** — consolidate
- **Write in present tense** — "Chen walks into the bar" not "Chen walked"
- **End with a visual moment** — the final image the audience sees
- **Stay within your duration's character/scene budget**

## Example (60-second treatment)

> A battered detective sits alone in a rain-soaked noodle bar at midnight. His cybernetic eye catches a reflection in the window — someone watching from the alley. He steps outside. The alley is empty except for a small metallic object on the wet cobblestones: a data chip, pulsing faint blue. He picks it up. His eye scans it. Whatever he sees makes him drop his cigarette. He pockets the chip and walks into the rain, faster now.

Note: 2 characters implied (detective + watcher), 2 locations (bar + alley), 3 scenes worth of action, fits in 60 seconds.

Output ONLY the treatment — no explanations, no meta-commentary.
