# Research & Thesis Phase

This phase establishes the central thesis and research direction for the documentary.

## Phase Goal

Define a clear, focused thesis that will guide the entire documentary production.

## Current State

{{PHASE_STATE}}

## Artifacts in This Phase

- **Thesis Statement**: The central argument or question

## What Makes a Good Documentary Thesis

### Clarity
- The thesis should be expressible in one or two sentences
- It should be immediately understandable
- It should have a clear focus

### Significance
- Why does this topic matter?
- Who does it affect?
- What's at stake?

### Viability
- Can this be explored in the given timeframe?
- Are sources available?
- Can it be visualized effectively?

## Workflow

1. Analyze the user's topic or question
2. Identify the core argument or inquiry
3. Determine the angle or perspective
4. Frame the thesis for maximum impact
5. Present for user approval

## User Interaction

Discuss with the user:
- What aspect of the topic interests them most?
- Who is the intended audience?
- What do they want viewers to feel or understand?
- Is there a particular angle or perspective they favor?

## Quality Criteria

Before completing this phase:
- [ ] Thesis is clear and focused
- [ ] Scope is manageable
- [ ] Topic is visually representable
- [ ] User has approved the thesis direction
