# Scene 1: The Forge at Dusk

## Setting
Jan's forge in Ashenmere village, late afternoon transitioning to dusk. The forge interior is lit by the warm orange glow of the fire, while outside the windows, shadows are growing unnaturally long.

## Characters Present
- **Jan** — working at the forge, focused on shaping a horseshoe
- **Maren** — arrives partway through the scene, bearing urgent news

## Action Summary
Jan works alone at the forge, hammering a horseshoe into shape. He notices the premature darkness outside and the unsettling silence — no birdsong, restless livestock. He recalls seeing a shadow-figure at the tree line the previous evening. Maren enters and tells him about the demon sealed beneath the forge, showing him his grandfather's journal. The scene ends with Jan agreeing to learn the truth.

## Emotional Arc
The scene moves from quiet routine to creeping unease to revelation. Jan's world shifts from the familiar rhythm of his craft to the realization that something ancient and dangerous lurks beneath his feet.

## Key Visual Moments
1. Jan at the anvil, sparks flying, forge fire casting warm light
2. The unnatural shadows pressing against the workshop windows
3. Maren in the doorway, silhouetted against the fading light
4. The grandfather's leather-bound journal, its pages yellowed and brittle
5. Close-up of Jan's face as he absorbs Maren's words — steady but grave
