# Setting Profile: Ashenmere Village

## Overview
Ashenmere is a small, remote mountain village nestled in a valley between two forested ridges. It is home to roughly sixty people — farmers, herders, a tavern keeper, and a blacksmith. The village is largely self-sufficient, with trade caravans visiting only a few times a year.

## Visual Description
The village consists of stone-and-timber buildings with slate roofs, arranged along a single main road that follows the Mirren creek. Smoke rises from chimneys in the cool mountain air. The forge sits at the western end of the village, its stone walls blackened by generations of soot. A small stone chapel with a wooden bell tower stands at the center. The surrounding landscape is rugged — dense pine forests climb the ridges, and the mountain peaks are often shrouded in mist.

## Atmosphere
Ashenmere has a quiet, timeless quality. The air smells of pine resin, wood smoke, and damp earth. Sounds carry far in the valley — the ring of Jan's hammer, the bleating of goats, the creak of the old stone bridge. As the shadow demon's influence grows, the atmosphere shifts: shadows deepen unnaturally, birdsong ceases, and an oppressive silence settles over the village at dusk.

## Key Locations
- **The Forge:** The oldest building in the village, built by Jan's grandfather atop a deep foundation stone that serves as a seal.
- **The Tavern (Eska's):** A warm, low-ceilinged gathering place where villagers share news and rumors.
- **The Chapel:** A modest stone building used for village meetings as much as worship.
- **The Stone Bridge:** An ancient arched bridge over the Mirren creek, marking the eastern entrance to the village.
- **The Tree Line:** The edge of the dense pine forest where the shadow demon first manifests.

## Time Period
Pre-industrial fantasy setting. No electricity, no firearms. Tools, clothing, and architecture reflect a late medieval European village.
